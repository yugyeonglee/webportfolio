About Me

Hi! My name is Yugyeong Lee. I was born in Korea but grew up in the southern part of Texas. I graduated from Sharyland High School in 2015 and am currently studying architecture in Carnegie Mellon University as a first year student.

I am fascinated by the impact of architecture in human culture and how architecture involves multiple intelligences to work simultaneously in order to create an effective design. Aside from architecture, I have a strong passtion towards music.

Tugendhat House

Bass wood model of Tugendhat House by Mies Van Der Rohe.

Collaborated with Camila Son, Eric Chen, and Dan Cascaval.

Phenomenon: Cube House

Texture Mapping of Cube House through the use of Sketchup and Photoshop.

Inspired by BIG - Bjarke Ingels Group + OFF's rendered image of project called Paris which reflects utilization of a perspective view of the building and implication of colorful light that contributes to a sense of monumental and experiential feeling.

Still Life

Still Life using graphite, turpentine oil, and oil paint.

Utilized multi-media to represent different types of surfaces presented in the still life.

Sections of Theater in Montreuil

Sections of the Teater in Montreuil by Dominique Coulon.

Inspired by uitilization of light line weight to achieve a less graphic section drawing overall but maintain depth.

Hands

Combination of use of acrylic paint, construction paper, and threads.

Utilized heavy use of acrylic paint to articulate variety of colors in a hand.

Inspired by kinetic movement of a hand and its connection with nature.

Analytical Diagrams: Cube House

Analytical Diagrams of the Cube House to show different utilization of space by exploring role of light and breeze, theme of private v.s public, and circulation of the Cube House.

Inspired by diagrams on Coolsingel by OMA to reflect sequential representation of the cube house.
